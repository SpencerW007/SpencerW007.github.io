Before High School

I went to elementary school at Park Hill Elementary which is located in Denver. I was there from kindergarten through 5th grade. After that, I went to DCIS for middle school which is an abbreviation of Denvcer Center for International Studies. This was mainly a language school where I spent one year learning Lakota and another year learning Italian. I went here for 6th and 7th grade. At the end of my 7th grade, I was told I could skip 8th grade because of my grades and test scores. So I skipped 8th grade and went straight to highschool.

High School

After middle school, I went to highschool at Denver East High School located in central Denver. While I was there, I was mainly studying engineeirng courses such as AP calculus BC, AP physics, and AP chemistry. However, once I entered my senior year, I realized I really didn't enjoy these courses so met with my counselor. After that, I decided to switch to business classes such as accounting, AP statistics, and social media marketing. I enjoyed these classes a lot more and tried out DECA which is a business club of sorts. I went to state after my first year doing it. From here, I realized I wanted to do business as a college degree. I applied to the CU Leeds School of Business and got in which has led me to where I am today.

College

When I got to college I knew I wanted to do business, but was unsure about what emphasis. I started with entrepreneurship and marketing. But I quickly realized I was more into the creative side of marketing and not the research side. So I applied for creative technology and design (CTD) minor to focus on the creative side of marketing that I enjoy. I also interned at startup which made me decide I didn't enjoy the entrepreneurship journey as much as I thought I would've. From here I was unsure what I wanted to do. Sophomore year I took the business modules - short 8 week courses that do a quick run of different emphasises of business. After this, I realized I enjoyed operations management and information management. I decided to major in these two areas, and then added a third major of business analytics because it had a lot of similarities with the other two. I also found out that there was a business of sports certificate, and I am a huge sports fan - especially soccer. I applied for this and got in, so decided to add that to my list of things to study. That leads me to today where I am finsishing up my senior year and then going out to Phoenix for a job as a merchant trainiee.
